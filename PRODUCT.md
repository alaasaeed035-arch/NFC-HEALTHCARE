# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary: Clinical staff (doctors, receptionists, pharmacists) using the system during active shifts — queuing patients, reviewing prescriptions, managing inventory, and dispensing medication. Speed and clarity matter; errors have real consequences.

Secondary: Patients logging in from home to view their medical history, registered NFC card, and health passport. They need reassurance and simplicity, not density.

## Product Purpose

An NFC-based healthcare card system that lets patients register once and receive coordinated care across multiple hospitals. The card acts as a portable health identity — clinical staff scan it to instantly access a patient's records, queue them, prescribe, or dispense medication. The system unifies patient flow across roles and facilities.

## Brand Personality

Calm, reliable, efficient. The interface should feel like a well-run hospital — structured without being cold, fast without feeling rushed, trustworthy without being bureaucratic.

## Anti-references

- Consumer health apps (MyFitnessPal, Apple Health) — too flashy and playful for a clinical context
- Government portals — cold, rigid, visually punishing
- Generic startup SaaS dashboards — heavy gradients, hero metrics, hollow visual flair
- Anything that feels more like a landing page than a tool

## Design Principles

1. **Legibility first.** Clinical data is the product. No decoration competes with information.
2. **Trust through consistency.** Same patterns across all roles reduce cognitive load and prevent errors in high-stakes moments.
3. **Role-appropriate density.** Staff views can carry more data; patient views need calm and simplicity.
4. **Calm confidence.** The interface should feel stable and in control, especially when handling sensitive records.
5. **Efficiency without friction.** Staff workflows should be completable with minimal clicks; the system should anticipate the next step.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline. Good contrast ratios throughout. Colorblind-safe palette (no red/green-only signaling). Clear typographic hierarchy. Reduced motion support where animations are used.
